One very long first date

St. Louis · the summer we met

We met in Missouri the summer Juli was home for grad school. Josh offered to plan the whole first date, then quietly turned it into a marathon: brunch and Aperol spritzes, thrifting down Cherokee Street, a gloriously bad wax museum, a walk under the Arch, dinner, and skee-ball to seal it. Nine hours in, neither of us wanted to go home.

home, plants and all

A life full of plants (and one cat)

Washington, DC · our life now

Somewhere between the tacos and the arcade tickets, that one date became everything. We built a life together in DC: a small apartment slowly and happily overtaken by houseplants, one very opinionated and very photogenic cat, and a windowsill that is honestly more greenhouse than window.

the yes, under a million lights

He asked under a million lights

Missouri Botanical Garden · winter 2025

That December, Josh brought Juli to Garden Glow at the Missouri Botanical Garden, the one with the great glass Climatron dome. Somewhere in a field of a million twinkling lights he got down on one knee, and Juli said yes. Engaged in a garden, of course, it could not have been anywhere else.

peak bloom, our real save-the-dates

And now, home to marry

The Tidal Basin · every spring

Every spring we stand under the cherry blossoms at the Tidal Basin, and this year we brought a photographer, so yes, those pink save-the-date photos are the real thing. Now we are heading home to St. Louis to marry under the glass of The Jewel Box, surrounded by calla lilies and every person we love.
