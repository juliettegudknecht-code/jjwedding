02 · The rise

What is causing the rise in primary identification of autism in special education?

No single thing. Researchers point to broadened diagnostic criteria, earlier and wider screening, and less stigma, alongside diagnostic substitution: some students who years ago would have been counted under intellectual disability or a specific learning disability are now identified as autistic. The trend line below shows the fingerprint of that shift. As autism climbed, both of those older categories declined.

Autism's share has grown nearly tenfold since 2000.

In 2000–01, autism was 1.5% of students with disabilities. By 2024–25 it is about 1 in 7 (14.9%). Over the same years, specific learning disability fell from 45% to 31% and intellectual disability from 10% to 5%. When one category climbs while the two it overlaps with fall, that is the signature of changing identification, not a sudden change in children. Sources: NCES Digest of Education Statistics 2023, table 204.30 (2000–01, and 2008–09 through 2022–23), and the OSEP IDEA Part B child count (2023–24 and 2024–25). All are child-count figures for the 50 states and DC.

Where they learn

Where do students spend the school day?

Federal law favors the "least restrictive environment." Across all disabilities, more than two-thirds are included for most of the day.

In 2023–24, schools logged more than two million disciplinary removals of students with disabilities. Here is the pace, and how it is distributed.

Removals reach students with disabilities at higher rates.

Students with disabilities are over-represented at every level of school discipline, and it starts before kindergarten. Each dot is one of 100 children who were punished; the glowing red is how far past their fair share they land.

06 · About the data

How to read these numbers honestly.

The big national count here is children served under IDEA Part B, ages 3 to 21, summed from the federal child-count file, and it includes the 50 states, DC, and the nine additional reporting entities (the territories and the freely associated states). The state-by-state views later in the story cover the 50 states and DC, so those figures compare cleanly across years.
