The glass house, since 1936

The Jewel Box was dedicated on November 14, 1936, designed by city engineer William C. E. Becker and financed in part by the federal Public Works Administration. Becker's design broke with greenhouse convention: instead of sloping the glass, he stepped the fifty-foot walls in vertical tiers, so Missouri hail glances off the panes rather than crashing through them. More than 16,000 square feet of plate glass wraps the display house and its lily pools.

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2000 and restored from glass to floor in 2002, it remains one of the most sought-after wedding venues in St. Louis. Its floral calendar has hardly changed in ninety years: poinsettias each December, lilies each spring, and tropical green in every season between.
