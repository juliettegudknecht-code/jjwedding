The calla, Juli's favorite flower

Calla comes from the Greek word for beauty, and honestly, fair.

It isn't a true lily at all. It's a Zantedeschia, a marsh plant that grew up along streams in southern Africa before it charmed its way into every greenhouse on earth.

The white sweep isn't a petal either. It's one perfect leaf, curled around that little golden torch of a bloom.

In the language of flowers it means faithfulness and magnificent beauty, which is why it keeps getting invited to weddings.

and it comes back every year, which is rather the point
